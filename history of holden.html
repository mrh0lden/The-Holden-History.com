The History of Holden

Holden was an iconic Australian automotive brand that played a significant role in the nation's car industry. Established in 1856, Holden originally started as a saddlery manufacturer before transforming into a major player in the automotive world. This website provides a look into Holden's rich history.

Timeline of Holden's Key Events

1856 - Founding of Holden

Holden was founded by James Alexander Holden in Adelaide as a saddlery company. The business quickly grew, and by 1908, Holden began to transition towards the automotive industry.

1924 - Holden’s First Car Production

Holden's first major automotive production began with the creation of a car body for General Motors (GM). This was a key moment in Holden's journey toward becoming a prominent car manufacturer.

1948 - Introduction of the Holden 48-215

In 1948, Holden released the 48-215, the first car fully designed and built in Australia. The 48-215 was also known as the 'FX' model and was considered a major milestone for the Australian automotive industry.

1960s - Expansion and Popularity

The 1960s saw Holden become a household name across Australia. With iconic models like the EH, HK, and Monaro, Holden became synonymous with Australian car culture during this decade.

1970s - The Rise of the Commodore

The 1970s marked the arrival of the Holden Commodore, one of the most successful and beloved cars in Holden’s history. The Commodore became synonymous with Australian family cars and was a flagship model for the brand for decades.

2000s - Transition to International Markets

In the 2000s, Holden expanded its presence in international markets and continued to innovate with new models. Despite challenges from both local and overseas competitors, Holden remained a dominant force in the Australian automotive industry.

2017 - Announcement of Holden’s Closure

In 2017, General Motors announced the closure of Holden's Australian manufacturing operations, marking the end of an era for the iconic brand. While Holden vehicles continued to be sold in Australia, the manufacturing of these cars was now outsourced to overseas markets.

2021 - The Final Chapter

In 2021, Holden officially ended its operations in Australia, marking the end of the brand's 160-year legacy in the Australian automotive market. The decision to cease operations sparked a range of emotions from loyal Holden fans and car enthusiasts around the country.
